About the name:

Who we are

We use licensed SAP Software (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SAP_SE) for projects from our German clients. We maintain a big repository of SAP translations. Unlike generic computer terms, SAP German-English terminology is slightly different. It is not what one normally thinks of. For example, document parking is a very specific term in SAP, which in SAP technically means temporarily saving the data. Here we can neither say "saving the document" nor "pre-creation of document". Once you are familiar with the SAP tool, you would have to say "parking" instead of "temporarily storing" or "pre-creation". Coming to German translation, to park generally means "parken" in German language, which is wrong if you use this in SAP works. It should be: vorerfassen.

Another example: The German word "Preishistorie" normally means "price history" in English. But in SAP it is called "cost change history". Though you have basic German knowledge, SAP terminologies are different from standard German. You've seen one, you've seen them all ! You can rely on this website, to avoid wasting time in searching for the exact SAP meaning.
